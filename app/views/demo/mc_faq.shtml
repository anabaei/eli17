    10. I installed Office 2010 and now my e-mail inbox is filling up with e-mails from Microsoft.

    This issue may be caused by “RSS Feeds”. RSS Feeds (Really Simple Syndication) are a new feature in Outlook 2007 and provide a summary of data from numerous sources of web content in a single output. In some cases the “Microsoft at Work” and “Microsoft at Home” RSS Feeds subscriptions are already setup in your Outlook resulting in Microsoft e-mails arriving in your inbox.

    To remove an RSS Feed:

    1. Open Outlook.

    2. Under the Mail Folders, find the folder entitled "RSSFeeds" (generally listed under the Outbox).

    3. Click to expand the RSS Folder.

    4. Select the RSS Feed that you want to delete.

    5. Press Delete.

    6. When prompted to confirm, click "Yes".

    For your convenience, we suggest that you complete the following steps before going home to download Office Professional Plus 2010:

    • Click on the “Download” button.
    • Enter your LDB e-mail to check that you qualify for this offer.
    • If you are asked for a Program Code, enter 2EF1E902CF.
    • Microsoft will send you an e-mail with the link to their Home Use Program login as well as your account information and password for downloading the suite. You may want to forward this e-mail to your personal e-mail account that you receive from Microsoft.

    When at home and ready to download the suite onto your home PC:

    • Remember to enter “idir\” directly in front of your IDIR ID.

    17. If I download the software and then get a new PC, can I do the download again?

    Yes. According to the Microsoft End User Licence Agreement, you may reassign the licence to a different device any number of times, but not more than one time every 90 days. If you reassign, that new device becomes the “licensed device.” If you retire the licensed device due to hardware failure, you may reassign the licence sooner.

    18. How do I transfer Office Professional Plus 2010 from my old PC to my new PC?

    Locate the Office Professional Plus 2010 application files that you downloaded on to your original PC, as well as your Product Key. As per our advice on "How do I download Office Professional Plus 2010" you should have a folder containing all the downloaded files for Office 2010 as well as a document where you recorded the product key. Copy this folder onto a disk and then install it on your new PC. When you have finished, remember to uninstall from your old machine, as per Microsoft's End User Licence Agreement.
